# 09. Final verdict

## The answer

GlobiGuard should become:

**the trust control plane for AI execution**

or more concretely:

**the authority infrastructure that enterprises insert between AI intent and real-world action.**

## Not these businesses

It should not primarily be:

- a library company
- a workflow-node company
- a generic automation builder
- a compliance report company
- an AI wrapper company

## The business it should actually build

### Product center

- policy authority
- action authorization
- human review orchestration
- audit and evidence
- deployment identity
- trust-boundary enforcement

### Revenue center

- hosted control plane
- private managed tenant
- self-hosted enterprise
- premium governance workflows
- regulated-industry packs
- support and SLAs

### Distribution center

- open SDKs
- adapters
- workflow nodes
- reference tooling
- docs and onboarding

## The long-term strategic bet

The winning bet is not:

"more features around AI"

It is:

"be the place organizations cannot remove once AI is touching real data and real actions."

## Why this has longevity

Models change.  
Workflow tools change.  
Agent frameworks change.  
Compliance fashions change.

But these questions do not go away:

1. what may the AI see?
2. what may the AI do?
3. when must a human review?
4. what proof exists afterward?
5. where did the sensitive data stay?

The company that owns those questions owns a durable category.

## Final recommendation

### Open source

Open the adoption layer.

### Commercialize

Commercialize the authority layer.

### Positioning

Sell GlobiGuard as critical infrastructure, not as a developer convenience.

### Sequence

Use the libraries to spread.  
Use the control plane to win.  
Use enterprise trust to endure.
