# 05. What not to become

## 1. Do not become a compliance cosmetics company

If the center of gravity becomes:

- reports
- certificates
- dashboards without enforcement

then the product gets pushed toward presentation value instead of operational value.

## 2. Do not become a generic workflow builder

The world does not need another broad automation canvas with weak differentiation.

If GlobiGuard tries to beat n8n, Zapier, or Make at general workflow creation, it will dilute the stronger position.

The better role is:

**the authority layer that makes those workflows safe and governable**

## 3. Do not become a thin SDK company

Thin wrappers are easy to replace.

If the product can be copied by replacing a package import, the business will not hold.

## 4. Do not become mostly services-led

Services may help early sales, but if every deployment needs heavy custom work, scale and margin both suffer.

The product must keep moving toward repeatable control-plane behavior.

## 5. Do not chase "AI safety" as a vague umbrella

That language is too broad and too soft.

The sharper position is:

- governed execution
- trust-boundary enforcement
- policy authority
- human review orchestration
- compliance evidence

## Hard rule

GlobiGuard should win by becoming **necessary infrastructure**, not by becoming a bundle of useful extras.
