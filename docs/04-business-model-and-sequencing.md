# 04. Business model and sequencing

## Core business model

GlobiGuard should monetize in three layers.

## Layer 1 - Adoption layer

### What it is

Open distribution.

### Includes

- SDKs
- React package
- n8n package
- docs
- examples
- local bootstrap tooling

### Goal

Maximize insertion into real workflows.

### Economic role

This layer creates reach, trust, and pipeline.  
It should not be expected to carry the business by itself.

---

## Layer 2 - Control plane revenue

### What it is

The hosted or private managed GlobiGuard authority layer.

### Includes

- policy management
- project and environment identity
- approvals
- audit and evidence
- deployment visibility
- key issuance and rotation
- workflow governance

### Why it matters

This is the recurring revenue center.

This is where GlobiGuard becomes hard to replace.

---

## Layer 3 - Enterprise authority revenue

### What it is

High-value deployment and governance capabilities for serious customers.

### Includes

- self-hosted enterprise
- private single-tenant managed
- hybrid governance
- sovereign or air-gapped programs
- premium policy packs
- premium connectors
- compliance mappings
- support and SLAs

### Why it matters

This is where margin and longevity come from.

## Best initial monetization path

Before phases, there is one strategic correction:

**Do not try to monetize the packages first.**

Packages are the spread mechanism.  
The authority layer is the revenue mechanism.

### Phase 1

Sell **private managed tenant** and **self-hosted enterprise** first.

Why:

- strongest pain
- clearest budget owners
- highest trust premium
- most aligned with the architecture

### Phase 2

Expand into workflow and developer distribution:

- Node SDK
- n8n package
- React package

but keep those tied back to the authority layer.

### Phase 3

Expand product depth:

- governance analytics
- benchmark and policy intelligence
- approval optimization
- industry packs
- model and connector governance

## Revenue logic

The strongest pricing axes are likely:

1. environments or tenants
2. governed actions or workflow volume
3. connectors and integrations
4. advanced compliance and enterprise controls
5. support tier

## Category rule

If GlobiGuard ever has to choose between:

- improving package adoption
- deepening the authority layer

it should usually choose the second **unless** the adoption surface is a bottleneck to entering real customer workflows.

## What not to optimize for

- one-time package sales
- consulting-heavy customization as the main business
- PDF output as the center of value
- feature sprawl before control-plane depth

## Strategic test

A good feature makes GlobiGuard:

- more central
- more trusted
- more operationally necessary
- more embedded in approvals, policies, and evidence

If a feature only makes the demo prettier or the wrapper easier, but does not deepen authority, it is secondary.
